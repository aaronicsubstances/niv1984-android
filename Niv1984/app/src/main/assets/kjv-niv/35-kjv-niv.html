Habakkuk 1

  1. (NIV) The oracle that Habakkuk the prophet received.
  1. (KJV) The burden which Habakkuk the prophet did see.
  1. (NIV) How long, O LORD, must I call for help, but you do not listen? Or cry out to you, "Violence!" but you do not save?
  1. (KJV) O LORD, how long shall I cry, and thou wilt not hear! even cry out unto thee of violence, and thou wilt not save!
  1. (NIV) Why do you make me look at injustice? Why do you tolerate wrong? Destruction and violence are before me; there is strife, and conflict abounds.
  1. (KJV) Why dost thou shew me iniquity, and cause me to behold grievance? for spoiling and violence are before me: and there are that raise up strife and contention.
  1. (NIV) Therefore the law is paralyzed, and justice never prevails. The wicked hem in the righteous, so that justice is perverted.
  1. (KJV) Therefore the law is slacked, and judgment doth never go forth: for the wicked doth compass about the righteous; therefore wrong judgment proceedeth.
  1. (NIV) "Look at the nations and watch-- and be utterly amazed. For I am going to do something in your days that you would not believe, even if you were told.
  1. (KJV) Behold ye among the heathen, and regard, and wonder marvellously: for I will work a work in your days, which ye will not believe, though it be told you.
  1. (NIV) I am raising up the Babylonians, [1] that ruthless and impetuous people, who sweep across the whole earth to seize dwelling places not their own.
  1. (KJV) For, lo, I raise up the Chaldeans, that bitter and hasty nation, which shall march through the breadth of the land, to possess the dwellingplaces that are not theirs.
  1. (NIV) They are a feared and dreaded people; they are a law to themselves and promote their own honor.
  1. (KJV) They are terrible and dreadful: their judgment and their dignity shall proceed of themselves.
  1. (NIV) Their horses are swifter than leopards, fiercer than wolves at dusk. Their cavalry gallops headlong; their horsemen come from afar. They fly like a vulture swooping to devour;
  1. (KJV) Their horses also are swifter than the leopards, and are more fierce than the evening wolves: and their horsemen shall spread themselves, and their horsemen shall come from far; they shall fly as the eagle that hasteth to eat.
  1. (NIV) they all come bent on violence. Their hordes [2] advance like a desert wind and gather prisoners like sand.
  1. (KJV) They shall come all for violence: their faces shall sup up as the east wind, and they shall gather the captivity as the sand.
  1. (NIV) They deride kings and scoff at rulers. They laugh at all fortified cities; they build earthen ramps and capture them.
  1. (KJV) And they shall scoff at the kings, and the princes shall be a scorn unto them: they shall deride every strong hold; for they shall heap dust, and take it.
  1. (NIV) Then they sweep past like the wind and go on-- guilty men, whose own strength is their god."
  1. (KJV) Then shall his mind change, and he shall pass over, and offend, imputing this his power unto his god.
  1. (NIV) O LORD, are you not from everlasting? My God, my Holy One, we will not die. O LORD, you have appointed them to execute judgment; O Rock, you have ordained them to punish.
  1. (KJV) Art thou not from everlasting, O LORD my God, mine Holy One? we shall not die. O LORD, thou hast ordained them for judgment; and, O mighty God, thou hast established them for correction.
  1. (NIV) Your eyes are too pure to look on evil; you cannot tolerate wrong. Why then do you tolerate the treacherous? Why are you silent while the wicked swallow up those more righteous than themselves?
  1. (KJV) Thou art of purer eyes than to behold evil, and canst not look on iniquity: wherefore lookest thou upon them that deal treacherously, and holdest thy tongue when the wicked devoureth the man that is more righteous than he?
  1. (NIV) You have made men like fish in the sea, like sea creatures that have no ruler.
  1. (KJV) And makest men as the fishes of the sea, as the creeping things, that have no ruler over them?
  1. (NIV) The wicked foe pulls all of them up with hooks, he catches them in his net, he gathers them up in his dragnet; and so he rejoices and is glad.
  1. (KJV) They take up all of them with the angle, they catch them in their net, and gather them in their drag: therefore they rejoice and are glad.
  1. (NIV) Therefore he sacrifices to his net and burns incense to his dragnet, for by his net he lives in luxury and enjoys the choicest food.
  1. (KJV) Therefore they sacrifice unto their net, and burn incense unto their drag; because by them their portion is fat, and their meat plenteous.
  1. (NIV) Is he to keep on emptying his net, destroying nations without mercy?
  1. (KJV) Shall they therefore empty their net, and not spare continually to slay the nations?

Habakkuk 2

  1. (NIV) I will stand at my watch and station myself on the ramparts; I will look to see what he will say to me, and what answer I am to give to this complaint. [1]
  1. (KJV) I will stand upon my watch, and set me upon the tower, and will watch to see what he will say unto me, and what I shall answer when I am reproved.
  1. (NIV) Then the LORD replied: "Write down the revelation and make it plain on tablets so that a herald [2] may run with it.
  1. (KJV) And the LORD answered me, and said, Write the vision, and make it plain upon tables, that he may run that readeth it.
  1. (NIV) For the revelation awaits an appointed time; it speaks of the end and will not prove false. Though it linger, wait for it; it [3] will certainly come and will not delay.
  1. (KJV) For the vision is yet for an appointed time, but at the end it shall speak, and not lie: though it tarry, wait for it; because it will surely come, it will not tarry.
  1. (NIV) "See, he is puffed up; his desires are not upright-- but the righteous will live by his faith [4] --
  1. (KJV) Behold, his soul which is lifted up is not upright in him: but the just shall live by his faith.
  1. (NIV) indeed, wine betrays him; he is arrogant and never at rest. Because he is as greedy as the grave [5] and like death is never satisfied, he gathers to himself all the nations and takes captive all the peoples.
  1. (KJV) Yea also, because he transgresseth by wine, he is a proud man, neither keepeth at home, who enlargeth his desire as hell, and is as death, and cannot be satisfied, but gathereth unto him all nations, and heapeth unto him all people:
  1. (NIV) "Will not all of them taunt him with ridicule and scorn, saying, "`Woe to him who piles up stolen goods and makes himself wealthy by extortion! How long must this go on?'
  1. (KJV) Shall not all these take up a parable against him, and a taunting proverb against him, and say, Woe to him that increaseth that which is not his! how long? and to him that ladeth himself with thick clay!
  1. (NIV) Will not your debtors [6] suddenly arise? Will they not wake up and make you tremble? Then you will become their victim.
  1. (KJV) Shall they not rise up suddenly that shall bite thee, and awake that shall vex thee, and thou shalt be for booties unto them?
  1. (NIV) Because you have plundered many nations, the peoples who are left will plunder you. For you have shed man's blood; you have destroyed lands and cities and everyone in them.
  1. (KJV) Because thou hast spoiled many nations, all the remnant of the people shall spoil thee; because of men's blood, and for the violence of the land, of the city, and of all that dwell therein.
  1. (NIV) "Woe to him who builds his realm by unjust gain to set his nest on high, to escape the clutches of ruin!
  1. (KJV) Woe to him that coveteth an evil covetousness to his house, that he may set his nest on high, that he may be delivered from the power of evil!
  1. (NIV) You have plotted the ruin of many peoples, shaming your own house and forfeiting your life.
  1. (KJV) Thou hast consulted shame to thy house by cutting off many people, and hast sinned against thy soul.
  1. (NIV) The stones of the wall will cry out, and the beams of the woodwork will echo it.
  1. (KJV) For the stone shall cry out of the wall, and the beam out of the timber shall answer it.
  1. (NIV) "Woe to him who builds a city with bloodshed and establishes a town by crime!
  1. (KJV) Woe to him that buildeth a town with blood, and stablisheth a city by iniquity!
  1. (NIV) Has not the LORD Almighty determined that the people's labor is only fuel for the fire, that the nations exhaust themselves for nothing?
  1. (KJV) Behold, is it not of the LORD of hosts that the people shall labour in the very fire, and the people shall weary themselves for very vanity?
  1. (NIV) For the earth will be filled with the knowledge of the glory of the LORD, as the waters cover the sea.
  1. (KJV) For the earth shall be filled with the knowledge of the glory of the LORD, as the waters cover the sea.
  1. (NIV) "Woe to him who gives drink to his neighbors, pouring it from the wineskin till they are drunk, so that he can gaze on their naked bodies.
  1. (KJV) Woe unto him that giveth his neighbour drink, that puttest thy bottle to him, and makest him drunken also, that thou mayest look on their nakedness!
  1. (NIV) You will be filled with shame instead of glory. Now it is your turn! Drink and be exposed [7] ! The cup from the LORD's right hand is coming around to you, and disgrace will cover your glory.
  1. (KJV) Thou art filled with shame for glory: drink thou also, and let thy foreskin be uncovered: the cup of the LORD's right hand shall be turned unto thee, and shameful spewing shall be on thy glory.
  1. (NIV) The violence you have done to Lebanon will overwhelm you, and your destruction of animals will terrify you. For you have shed man's blood; you have destroyed lands and cities and everyone in them.
  1. (KJV) For the violence of Lebanon shall cover thee, and the spoil of beasts, which made them afraid, because of men's blood, and for the violence of the land, of the city, and of all that dwell therein.
  1. (NIV) "Of what value is an idol, since a man has carved it? Or an image that teaches lies? For he who makes it trusts in his own creation; he makes idols that cannot speak.
  1. (KJV) What profiteth the graven image that the maker thereof hath graven it; the molten image, and a teacher of lies, that the maker of his work trusteth therein, to make dumb idols?
  1. (NIV) Woe to him who says to wood, `Come to life!' Or to lifeless stone, `Wake up!' Can it give guidance? It is covered with gold and silver; there is no breath in it.
  1. (KJV) Woe unto him that saith to the wood, Awake; to the dumb stone, Arise, it shall teach! Behold, it is laid over with gold and silver, and there is no breath at all in the midst of it.

  1. (KJV) But the LORD is in his holy temple: let all the earth keep silence before him.
