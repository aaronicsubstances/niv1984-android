2 Thessalonians 1

  1. (NIV) Paul, Silas [1] and Timothy, To the church of the Thessalonians in God our Father and the Lord Jesus Christ:
  1. (KJV) Paul, and Silvanus, and Timotheus, unto the church of the Thessalonians in God our Father and the Lord Jesus Christ:
  1. (NIV) Grace and peace to you from God the Father and the Lord Jesus Christ.
  1. (KJV) Grace unto you, and peace, from God our Father and the Lord Jesus Christ.
  1. (NIV) We ought always to thank God for you, brothers, and rightly so, because your faith is growing more and more, and the love every one of you has for each other is increasing.
  1. (KJV) We are bound to thank God always for you, brethren, as it is meet, because that your faith groweth exceedingly, and the charity of every one of you all toward each other aboundeth;
  1. (NIV) Therefore, among God's churches we boast about your perseverance and faith in all the persecutions and trials you are enduring.
  1. (KJV) So that we ourselves glory in you in the churches of God for your patience and faith in all your persecutions and tribulations that ye endure:
  1. (NIV) All this is evidence that God's judgment is right, and as a result you will be counted worthy of the kingdom of God, for which you are suffering.
  1. (KJV) Which is a manifest token of the righteous judgment of God, that ye may be counted worthy of the kingdom of God, for which ye also suffer:
  1. (NIV) God is just: He will pay back trouble to those who trouble you
  1. (KJV) Seeing it is a righteous thing with God to recompense tribulation to them that trouble you;
  1. (NIV) and give relief to you who are troubled, and to us as well. This will happen when the Lord Jesus is revealed from heaven in blazing fire with his powerful angels.
  1. (KJV) And to you who are troubled rest with us, when the Lord Jesus shall be revealed from heaven with his mighty angels,
  1. (NIV) He will punish those who do not know God and do not obey the gospel of our Lord Jesus.
  1. (KJV) In flaming fire taking vengeance on them that know not God, and that obey not the gospel of our Lord Jesus Christ:
  1. (NIV) They will be punished with everlasting destruction and shut out from the presence of the Lord and from the majesty of his power
  1. (KJV) Who shall be punished with everlasting destruction from the presence of the Lord, and from the glory of his power;
  1. (NIV) on the day he comes to be glorified in his holy people and to be marveled at among all those who have believed. This includes you, because you believed our testimony to you.
  1. (KJV) When he shall come to be glorified in his saints, and to be admired in all them that believe (because our testimony among you was believed) in that day.
  1. (NIV) With this in mind, we constantly pray for you, that our God may count you worthy of his calling, and that by his power he may fulfill every good purpose of yours and every act prompted by your faith.
  1. (KJV) Wherefore also we pray always for you, that our God would count you worthy of this calling, and fulfil all the good pleasure of his goodness, and the work of faith with power:
  1. (NIV) We pray this so that the name of our Lord Jesus may be glorified in you, and you in him, according to the grace of our God and the Lord Jesus Christ. [2]
  1. (KJV) That the name of our Lord Jesus Christ may be glorified in you, and ye in him, according to the grace of our God and the Lord Jesus Christ.
  1. [1] Greek Silvanus, a variant of Silas
  2. [12] Or God and Lord, Jesus Christ

2 Thessalonians 2

  1. (NIV) Concerning the coming of our Lord Jesus Christ and our being gathered to him, we ask you, brothers,
  1. (KJV) Now we beseech you, brethren, by the coming of our Lord Jesus Christ, and by our gathering together unto him,
  1. (NIV) not to become easily unsettled or alarmed by some prophecy, report or letter supposed to have come from us, saying that the day of the Lord has already come.
  1. (KJV) That ye be not soon shaken in mind, or be troubled, neither by spirit, nor by word, nor by letter as from us, as that the day of Christ is at hand.
  1. (NIV) Don't let anyone deceive you in any way, for that day will not come until the rebellion occurs and the man of lawlessness [1] is revealed, the man doomed to destruction.
  1. (KJV) Let no man deceive you by any means: for that day shall not come, except there come a falling away first, and that man of sin be revealed, the son of perdition;
  1. (NIV) He will oppose and will exalt himself over everything that is called God or is worshiped, so that he sets himself up in God's temple, proclaiming himself to be God.
  1. (KJV) Who opposeth and exalteth himself above all that is called God, or that is worshipped; so that he as God sitteth in the temple of God, shewing himself that he is God.
  1. (NIV) Don't you remember that when I was with you I used to tell you these things?
  1. (KJV) Remember ye not, that, when I was yet with you, I told you these things?
  1. (NIV) And now you know what is holding him back, so that he may be revealed at the proper time.
  1. (KJV) And now ye know what withholdeth that he might be revealed in his time.
  1. (NIV) For the secret power of lawlessness is already at work; but the one who now holds it back will continue to do so till he is taken out of the way.
  1. (KJV) For the mystery of iniquity doth already work: only he who now letteth will let, until he be taken out of the way.
  1. (NIV) And then the lawless one will be revealed, whom the Lord Jesus will overthrow with the breath of his mouth and destroy by the splendor of his coming.
  1. (KJV) And then shall that Wicked be revealed, whom the Lord shall consume with the spirit of his mouth, and shall destroy with the brightness of his coming:
  1. (NIV) The coming of the lawless one will be in accordance with the work of Satan displayed in all kinds of counterfeit miracles, signs and wonders,
  1. (KJV) Even him, whose coming is after the working of Satan with all power and signs and lying wonders,
  1. (NIV) and in every sort of evil that deceives those who are perishing. They perish because they refused to love the truth and so be saved.
  1. (KJV) And with all deceivableness of unrighteousness in them that perish; because they received not the love of the truth, that they might be saved.
  1. (NIV) For this reason God sends them a powerful delusion so that they will believe the lie
  1. (KJV) And for this cause God shall send them strong delusion, that they should believe a lie:
  1. (NIV) and so that all will be condemned who have not believed the truth but have delighted in wickedness.
  1. (KJV) That they all might be damned who believed not the truth, but had pleasure in unrighteousness.
  1. (NIV) But we ought always to thank God for you, brothers loved by the Lord, because from the beginning God chose you [2] to be saved through the sanctifying work of the Spirit and through belief in the truth.
  1. (KJV) But we are bound to give thanks alway to God for you, brethren beloved of the Lord, because God hath from the beginning chosen you to salvation through sanctification of the Spirit and belief of the truth:
  1. (NIV) He called you to this through our gospel, that you might share in the glory of our Lord Jesus Christ.
  1. (KJV) Whereunto he called you by our gospel, to the obtaining of the glory of our Lord Jesus Christ.
  1. (NIV) So then, brothers, stand firm and hold to the teachings [3] we passed on to you, whether by word of mouth or by letter.
  1. (KJV) Therefore, brethren, stand fast, and hold the traditions which ye have been taught, whether by word, or our epistle.
  1. (NIV) May our Lord Jesus Christ himself and God our Father, who loved us and by his grace gave us eternal encouragement and good hope,
  1. (KJV) Now our Lord Jesus Christ himself, and God, even our Father, which hath loved us, and hath given us everlasting consolation and good hope through grace,
  1. (NIV) encourage your hearts and strengthen you in every good deed and word.
  1. (KJV) Comfort your hearts, and stablish you in every good word and work.
  1. [3] Some manuscripts sin
  2. [13] Some manuscripts because God chose you as his firstfruits
  3. [15] Or traditions
