2 Timothy 1

  1. (NIV) Paul, an apostle of Christ Jesus by the will of God, according to the promise of life that is in Christ Jesus,
  1. (KJV) Paul, an apostle of Jesus Christ by the will of God, according to the promise of life which is in Christ Jesus,
  1. (NIV) To Timothy, my dear son: Grace, mercy and peace from God the Father and Christ Jesus our Lord.
  1. (KJV) To Timothy, my dearly beloved son: Grace, mercy, and peace, from God the Father and Christ Jesus our Lord.
  1. (NIV) I thank God, whom I serve, as my forefathers did, with a clear conscience, as night and day I constantly remember you in my prayers.
  1. (KJV) I thank God, whom I serve from my forefathers with pure conscience, that without ceasing I have remembrance of thee in my prayers night and day;
  1. (NIV) Recalling your tears, I long to see you, so that I may be filled with joy.
  1. (KJV) Greatly desiring to see thee, being mindful of thy tears, that I may be filled with joy;
  1. (NIV) I have been reminded of your sincere faith, which first lived in your grandmother Lois and in your mother Eunice and, I am persuaded, now lives in you also.
  1. (KJV) When I call to remembrance the unfeigned faith that is in thee, which dwelt first in thy grandmother Lois, and thy mother Eunice; and I am persuaded that in thee also.
  1. (NIV) For this reason I remind you to fan into flame the gift of God, which is in you through the laying on of my hands.
  1. (KJV) Wherefore I put thee in remembrance that thou stir up the gift of God, which is in thee by the putting on of my hands.
  1. (NIV) For God did not give us a spirit of timidity, but a spirit of power, of love and of self-discipline.
  1. (KJV) For God hath not given us the spirit of fear; but of power, and of love, and of a sound mind.
  1. (NIV) So do not be ashamed to testify about our Lord, or ashamed of me his prisoner. But join with me in suffering for the gospel, by the power of God,
  1. (KJV) Be not thou therefore ashamed of the testimony of our Lord, nor of me his prisoner: but be thou partaker of the afflictions of the gospel according to the power of God;
  1. (NIV) who has saved us and called us to a holy life--not because of anything we have done but because of his own purpose and grace. This grace was given us in Christ Jesus before the beginning of time,
  1. (KJV) Who hath saved us, and called us with an holy calling, not according to our works, but according to his own purpose and grace, which was given us in Christ Jesus before the world began,
  1. (NIV) but it has now been revealed through the appearing of our Savior, Christ Jesus, who has destroyed death and has brought life and immortality to light through the gospel.
  1. (KJV) But is now made manifest by the appearing of our Saviour Jesus Christ, who hath abolished death, and hath brought life and immortality to light through the gospel:
  1. (NIV) And of this gospel I was appointed a herald and an apostle and a teacher.
  1. (KJV) Whereunto I am appointed a preacher, and an apostle, and a teacher of the Gentiles.
  1. (NIV) That is why I am suffering as I am. Yet I am not ashamed, because I know whom I have believed, and am convinced that he is able to guard what I have entrusted to him for that day.
  1. (KJV) For the which cause I also suffer these things: nevertheless I am not ashamed: for I know whom I have believed, and am persuaded that he is able to keep that which I have committed unto him against that day.
  1. (NIV) What you heard from me, keep as the pattern of sound teaching, with faith and love in Christ Jesus.
  1. (KJV) Hold fast the form of sound words, which thou hast heard of me, in faith and love which is in Christ Jesus.
  1. (NIV) Guard the good deposit that was entrusted to you--guard it with the help of the Holy Spirit who lives in us.
  1. (KJV) That good thing which was committed unto thee keep by the Holy Ghost which dwelleth in us.
  1. (NIV) You know that everyone in the province of Asia has deserted me, including Phygelus and Hermogenes.
  1. (KJV) This thou knowest, that all they which are in Asia be turned away from me; of whom are Phygellus and Hermogenes.
  1. (NIV) May the Lord show mercy to the household of Onesiphorus, because he often refreshed me and was not ashamed of my chains.
  1. (KJV) The Lord give mercy unto the house of Onesiphorus; for he oft refreshed me, and was not ashamed of my chain:
  1. (NIV) On the contrary, when he was in Rome, he searched hard for me until he found me.
  1. (KJV) But, when he was in Rome, he sought me out very diligently, and found me.
  1. (NIV) May the Lord grant that he will find mercy from the Lord on that day! You know very well in how many ways he helped me in Ephesus.
  1. (KJV) The Lord grant unto him that he may find mercy of the Lord in that day: and in how many things he ministered unto me at Ephesus, thou knowest very well.

2 Timothy 2

  1. (NIV) You then, my son, be strong in the grace that is in Christ Jesus.
  1. (KJV) Thou therefore, my son, be strong in the grace that is in Christ Jesus.
  1. (NIV) And the things you have heard me say in the presence of many witnesses entrust to reliable men who will also be qualified to teach others.
  1. (KJV) And the things that thou hast heard of me among many witnesses, the same commit thou to faithful men, who shall be able to teach others also.
  1. (NIV) Endure hardship with us like a good soldier of Christ Jesus.
  1. (KJV) Thou therefore endure hardness, as a good soldier of Jesus Christ.
  1. (NIV) No one serving as a soldier gets involved in civilian affairs--he wants to please his commanding officer.
  1. (KJV) No man that warreth entangleth himself with the affairs of this life; that he may please him who hath chosen him to be a soldier.
  1. (NIV) Similarly, if anyone competes as an athlete, he does not receive the victor's crown unless he competes according to the rules.
  1. (KJV) And if a man also strive for masteries, yet is he not crowned, except he strive lawfully.
  1. (NIV) The hardworking farmer should be the first to receive a share of the crops.
  1. (KJV) The husbandman that laboureth must be first partaker of the fruits.
  1. (NIV) Reflect on what I am saying, for the Lord will give you insight into all this.
  1. (KJV) Consider what I say; and the Lord give thee understanding in all things.
  1. (NIV) Remember Jesus Christ, raised from the dead, descended from David. This is my gospel,
  1. (KJV) Remember that Jesus Christ of the seed of David was raised from the dead according to my gospel:
  1. (NIV) for which I am suffering even to the point of being chained like a criminal. But God's word is not chained.
  1. (KJV) Wherein I suffer trouble, as an evil doer, even unto bonds; but the word of God is not bound.
  1. (NIV) Therefore I endure everything for the sake of the elect, that they too may obtain the salvation that is in Christ Jesus, with eternal glory.
  1. (KJV) Therefore I endure all things for the elect's sakes, that they may also obtain the salvation which is in Christ Jesus with eternal glory.
  1. (NIV) Here is a trustworthy saying: If we died with him, we will also live with him;
  1. (KJV) It is a faithful saying: For if we be dead with him, we shall also live with him:
  1. (NIV) if we endure, we will also reign with him. If we disown him, he will also disown us;
  1. (KJV) If we suffer, we shall also reign with him: if we deny him, he also will deny us:
  1. (NIV) if we are faithless, he will remain faithful, for he cannot disown himself.
  1. (KJV) If we believe not, yet he abideth faithful: he cannot deny himself.
  1. (NIV) Keep reminding them of these things. Warn them before God against quarreling about words; it is of no value, and only ruins those who listen.
  1. (KJV) Of these things put them in remembrance, charging them before the Lord that they strive not about words to no profit, but to the subverting of the hearers.
  1. (NIV) Do your best to present yourself to God as one approved, a workman who does not need to be ashamed and who correctly handles the word of truth.
  1. (KJV) Study to shew thyself approved unto God, a workman that needeth not to be ashamed, rightly dividing the word of truth.
  1. (NIV) Avoid godless chatter, because those who indulge in it will become more and more ungodly.
  1. (KJV) But shun profane and vain babblings: for they will increase unto more ungodliness.
  1. (NIV) Their teaching will spread like gangrene. Among them are Hymenaeus and Philetus,
  1. (KJV) And their word will eat as doth a canker: of whom is Hymenaeus and Philetus;
  1. (NIV) who have wandered away from the truth. They say that the resurrection has already taken place, and they destroy the faith of some.
  1. (KJV) Who concerning the truth have erred, saying that the resurrection is past already; and overthrow the faith of some.
  1. (NIV) Nevertheless, God's solid foundation stands firm, sealed with this inscription: "The Lord knows those who are his," [1] and, "Everyone who confesses the name of the Lord must turn away from wickedness."
  1. (KJV) Nevertheless the foundation of God standeth sure, having this seal, The Lord knoweth them that are his. And, Let every one that nameth the name of Christ depart from iniquity.
  1. (NIV) In a large house there are articles not only of gold and silver, but also of wood and clay; some are for noble purposes and some for ignoble.
  1. (KJV) But in a great house there are not only vessels of gold and of silver, but also of wood and of earth; and some to honour, and some to dishonour.
  1. (NIV) If a man cleanses himself from the latter, he will be an instrument for noble purposes, made holy, useful to the Master and prepared to do any good work.
  1. (KJV) If a man therefore purge himself from these, he shall be a vessel unto honour, sanctified, and meet for the master's use, and prepared unto every good work.
  1. (NIV) Flee the evil desires of youth, and pursue righteousness, faith, love and peace, along with those who call on the Lord out of a pure heart.
  1. (KJV) Flee also youthful lusts: but follow righteousness, faith, charity, peace, with them that call on the Lord out of a pure heart.
  1. (NIV) Don't have anything to do with foolish and stupid arguments, because you know they produce quarrels.
  1. (KJV) But foolish and unlearned questions avoid, knowing that they do gender strifes.
  1. (NIV) And the Lord's servant must not quarrel; instead, he must be kind to everyone, able to teach, not resentful.
  1. (KJV) And the servant of the Lord must not strive; but be gentle unto all men, apt to teach, patient,
  1. (NIV) Those who oppose him he must gently instruct, in the hope that God will grant them repentance leading them to a knowledge of the truth,
  1. (KJV) In meekness instructing those that oppose themselves; if God peradventure will give them repentance to the acknowledging of the truth;
  1. (NIV) and that they will come to their senses and escape from the trap of the devil, who has taken them captive to do his will.
  1. (KJV) And that they may recover themselves out of the snare of the devil, who are taken captive by him at his will.

  1. (KJV) But continue thou in the things which thou hast learned and hast been assured of, knowing of whom thou hast learned them;

  1. (KJV) And that from a child thou hast known the holy scriptures, which are able to make thee wise unto salvation through faith which is in Christ Jesus.

  1. (KJV) All scripture is given by inspiration of God, and is profitable for doctrine, for reproof, for correction, for instruction in righteousness:
