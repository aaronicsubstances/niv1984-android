Titus 1

  1. (KJV) Paul, a servant of God, and an apostle of Jesus Christ, according to the faith of God's elect, and the acknowledging of the truth which is after godliness;
  1. (NIV) a faith and knowledge resting on the hope of eternal life, which God, who does not lie, promised before the beginning of time,
  1. (KJV) In hope of eternal life, which God, that cannot lie, promised before the world began;
  1. (NIV) and at his appointed season he brought his word to light through the preaching entrusted to me by the command of God our Savior,
  1. (KJV) But hath in due times manifested his word through preaching, which is committed unto me according to the commandment of God our Saviour;
  1. (NIV) To Titus, my true son in our common faith: Grace and peace from God the Father and Christ Jesus our Savior.
  1. (KJV) To Titus, mine own son after the common faith: Grace, mercy, and peace, from God the Father and the Lord Jesus Christ our Saviour.
  1. (NIV) The reason I left you in Crete was that you might straighten out what was left unfinished and appoint [1] elders in every town, as I directed you.
  1. (KJV) For this cause left I thee in Crete, that thou shouldest set in order the things that are wanting, and ordain elders in every city, as I had appointed thee:
  1. (NIV) An elder must be blameless, the husband of but one wife, a man whose children believe and are not open to the charge of being wild and disobedient.
  1. (KJV) If any be blameless, the husband of one wife, having faithful children not accused of riot or unruly.
  1. (NIV) Since an overseer [2] is entrusted with God's work, he must be blameless--not overbearing, not quick-tempered, not given to drunkenness, not violent, not pursuing dishonest gain.
  1. (KJV) For a bishop must be blameless, as the steward of God; not selfwilled, not soon angry, not given to wine, no striker, not given to filthy lucre;
  1. (NIV) Rather he must be hospitable, one who loves what is good, who is self-controlled, upright, holy and disciplined.
  1. (KJV) But a lover of hospitality, a lover of good men, sober, just, holy, temperate;
  1. (NIV) He must hold firmly to the trustworthy message as it has been taught, so that he can encourage others by sound doctrine and refute those who oppose it.
  1. (KJV) Holding fast the faithful word as he hath been taught, that he may be able by sound doctrine both to exhort and to convince the gainsayers.
  1. (NIV) For there are many rebellious people, mere talkers and deceivers, especially those of the circumcision group.
  1. (KJV) For there are many unruly and vain talkers and deceivers, specially they of the circumcision:
  1. (NIV) They must be silenced, because they are ruining whole households by teaching things they ought not to teach--and that for the sake of dishonest gain.
  1. (KJV) Whose mouths must be stopped, who subvert whole houses, teaching things which they ought not, for filthy lucre's sake.
  1. (NIV) Even one of their own prophets has said, "Cretans are always liars, evil brutes, lazy gluttons."
  1. (KJV) One of themselves, even a prophet of their own, said, The Cretians are alway liars, evil beasts, slow bellies.
  1. (NIV) This testimony is true. Therefore, rebuke them sharply, so that they will be sound in the faith
  1. (KJV) This witness is true. Wherefore rebuke them sharply, that they may be sound in the faith;
  1. (NIV) and will pay no attention to Jewish myths or to the commands of those who reject the truth.
  1. (KJV) Not giving heed to Jewish fables, and commandments of men, that turn from the truth.
  1. (NIV) To the pure, all things are pure, but to those who are corrupted and do not believe, nothing is pure. In fact, both their minds and consciences are corrupted.
  1. (KJV) Unto the pure all things are pure: but unto them that are defiled and unbelieving is nothing pure; but even their mind and conscience is defiled.
  1. (NIV) They claim to know God, but by their actions they deny him. They are detestable, disobedient and unfit for doing anything good.
  1. (KJV) They profess that they know God; but in works they deny him, being abominable, and disobedient, and unto every good work reprobate.

Titus 2

  1. (NIV) You must teach what is in accord with sound doctrine.
  1. (KJV) But speak thou the things which become sound doctrine:
  1. (NIV) Teach the older men to be temperate, worthy of respect, self-controlled, and sound in faith, in love and in endurance.
  1. (KJV) That the aged men be sober, grave, temperate, sound in faith, in charity, in patience.
  1. (NIV) Likewise, teach the older women to be reverent in the way they live, not to be slanderers or addicted to much wine, but to teach what is good.
  1. (KJV) The aged women likewise, that they be in behaviour as becometh holiness, not false accusers, not given to much wine, teachers of good things;
  1. (NIV) Then they can train the younger women to love their husbands and children,
  1. (KJV) That they may teach the young women to be sober, to love their husbands, to love their children,
  1. (NIV) to be self-controlled and pure, to be busy at home, to be kind, and to be subject to their husbands, so that no one will malign the word of God.
  1. (KJV) To be discreet, chaste, keepers at home, good, obedient to their own husbands, that the word of God be not blasphemed.
  1. (NIV) Similarly, encourage the young men to be self-controlled.
  1. (KJV) Young men likewise exhort to be sober minded.
  1. (NIV) In everything set them an example by doing what is good. In your teaching show integrity, seriousness
  1. (KJV) In all things shewing thyself a pattern of good works: in doctrine shewing uncorruptness, gravity, sincerity,
  1. (NIV) and soundness of speech that cannot be condemned, so that those who oppose you may be ashamed because they have nothing bad to say about us.
  1. (KJV) Sound speech, that cannot be condemned; that he that is of the contrary part may be ashamed, having no evil thing to say of you.
  1. (NIV) Teach slaves to be subject to their masters in everything, to try to please them, not to talk back to them,
  1. (KJV) Exhort servants to be obedient unto their own masters, and to please them well in all things; not answering again;
  1. (NIV) and not to steal from them, but to show that they can be fully trusted, so that in every way they will make the teaching about God our Savior attractive.
  1. (KJV) Not purloining, but shewing all good fidelity; that they may adorn the doctrine of God our Saviour in all things.
  1. (NIV) For the grace of God that brings salvation has appeared to all men.
  1. (KJV) For the grace of God that bringeth salvation hath appeared to all men,
  1. (NIV) It teaches us to say "No" to ungodliness and worldly passions, and to live self-controlled, upright and godly lives in this present age,
  1. (KJV) Teaching us that, denying ungodliness and worldly lusts, we should live soberly, righteously, and godly, in this present world;
  1. (NIV) while we wait for the blessed hope--the glorious appearing of our great God and Savior, Jesus Christ,
  1. (KJV) Looking for that blessed hope, and the glorious appearing of the great God and our Saviour Jesus Christ;
  1. (NIV) who gave himself for us to redeem us from all wickedness and to purify for himself a people that are his very own, eager to do what is good.
  1. (KJV) Who gave himself for us, that he might redeem us from all iniquity, and purify unto himself a peculiar people, zealous of good works.
  1. (NIV) These, then, are the things you should teach. Encourage and rebuke with all authority. Do not let anyone despise you.
  1. (KJV) These things speak, and exhort, and rebuke with all authority. Let no man despise thee.
