Chapter 1

[1 NIV] Paul, a prisoner of Christ Jesus, and Timothy our brother, To Philemon our dear friend and fellow worker,
[1 KJV] Paul, a prisoner of Jesus Christ, and Timothy our brother, unto Philemon our dearly beloved, and fellowlabourer,
[2 NIV] to Apphia our sister, to Archippus our fellow soldier and to the church that meets in your home:
[2 KJV] And to our beloved Apphia, and Archippus our fellowsoldier, and to the church in thy house:
[3 NIV] Grace to you and peace from God our Father and the Lord Jesus Christ.
[3 KJV] Grace to you, and peace, from God our Father and the Lord Jesus Christ.
[4 NIV] I always thank my God as I remember you in my prayers,
[4 KJV] I thank my God, making mention of thee always in my prayers,
[5 NIV] because I hear about your faith in the Lord Jesus and your love for all the saints.
[5 KJV] Hearing of thy love and faith, which thou hast toward the Lord Jesus, and toward all saints;
[6 NIV] I pray that you may be active in sharing your faith, so that you will have a full understanding of every good thing we have in Christ.
[6 KJV] That the communication of thy faith may become effectual by the acknowledging of every good thing which is in you in Christ Jesus.
[7 NIV] Your love has given me great joy and encouragement, because you, brother, have refreshed the hearts of the saints.
[7 KJV] For we have great joy and consolation in thy love, because the bowels of the saints are refreshed by thee, brother.
[8 NIV] Therefore, although in Christ I could be bold and order you to do what you ought to do,
[8 KJV] Wherefore, though I might be much bold in Christ to enjoin thee that which is convenient,
[9 NIV] yet I appeal to you on the basis of love. I then, as Paul--an old man and now also a prisoner of Christ Jesus--
[9 KJV] Yet for love's sake I rather beseech thee, being such an one as Paul the aged, and now also a prisoner of Jesus Christ.
[10 NIV] I appeal to you for my son Onesimus, [1] who became my son while I was in chains.
[10 KJV] I beseech thee for my son Onesimus, whom I have begotten in my bonds:
[11 NIV] Formerly he was useless to you, but now he has become useful both to you and to me.
[11 KJV] Which in time past was to thee unprofitable, but now profitable to thee and to me:
[12 NIV] I am sending him--who is my very heart--back to you.
[12 KJV] Whom I have sent again: thou therefore receive him, that is, mine own bowels:
[13 NIV] I would have liked to keep him with me so that he could take your place in helping me while I am in chains for the gospel.
[13 KJV] Whom I would have retained with me, that in thy stead he might have ministered unto me in the bonds of the gospel:
[14 NIV] But I did not want to do anything without your consent, so that any favor you do will be spontaneous and not forced.
[14 KJV] But without thy mind would I do nothing; that thy benefit should not be as it were of necessity, but willingly.
[15 NIV] Perhaps the reason he was separated from you for a little while was that you might have him back for good--
[15 KJV] For perhaps he therefore departed for a season, that thou shouldest receive him for ever;
[16 NIV] no longer as a slave, but better than a slave, as a dear brother. He is very dear to me but even dearer to you, both as a man and as a brother in the Lord.
[16 KJV] Not now as a servant, but above a servant, a brother beloved, specially to me, but how much more unto thee, both in the flesh, and in the Lord?
[17 NIV] So if you consider me a partner, welcome him as you would welcome me.
[17 KJV] If thou count me therefore a partner, receive him as myself.
[18 NIV] If he has done you any wrong or owes you anything, charge it to me.
[18 KJV] If he hath wronged thee, or oweth thee ought, put that on mine account;
[19 NIV] I, Paul, am writing this with my own hand. I will pay it back--not to mention that you owe me your very self.
[19 KJV] I Paul have written it with mine own hand, I will repay it: albeit I do not say to thee how thou owest unto me even thine own self besides.
[20 NIV] I do wish, brother, that I may have some benefit from you in the Lord; refresh my heart in Christ.
[20 KJV] Yea, brother, let me have joy of thee in the Lord: refresh my bowels in the Lord.
[21 NIV] Confident of your obedience, I write to you, knowing that you will do even more than I ask.
[21 KJV] Having confidence in thy obedience I wrote unto thee, knowing that thou wilt also do more than I say.
[22 NIV] And one thing more: Prepare a guest room for me, because I hope to be restored to you in answer to your prayers.
[22 KJV] But withal prepare me also a lodging: for I trust that through your prayers I shall be given unto you.
[23 NIV] Epaphras, my fellow prisoner in Christ Jesus, sends you greetings.
[23 KJV] There salute thee Epaphras, my fellowprisoner in Christ Jesus;
[24 NIV] And so do Mark, Aristarchus, Demas and Luke, my fellow workers.
[24 KJV] Marcus, Aristarchus, Demas, Lucas, my fellowlabourers.
[25 NIV] The grace of the Lord Jesus Christ be with your spirit.
[25 KJV] The grace of our Lord Jesus Christ be with your spirit. Amen.
1. [10] Onesimus means useful.
