  1. In the past God spoke to our forefathers through the prophets at many times and in various ways,
  1. but in these last days he has spoken to us by his Son, whom he appointed heir of all things, and through whom he made the universe.
  1. The Son is the radiance of God's glory and the exact representation of his being, sustaining all things by his powerful word. After he had provided purification for sins, he sat down at the right hand of the Majesty in heaven.
  1. So he became as much superior to the angels as the name he has inherited is superior to theirs.

Hebrews 7

  1. This Melchizedek was king of Salem and priest of God Most High. He met Abraham returning from the defeat of the kings and blessed him,
  1. and Abraham gave him a tenth of everything. First, his name means "king of righteousness"; then also, "king of Salem" means "king of peace."
  1. Without father or mother, without genealogy, without beginning of days or end of life, like the Son of God he remains a priest forever.
  1. Just think how great he was: Even the patriarch Abraham gave him a tenth of the plunder!
  1. Now the law requires the descendants of Levi who become priests to collect a tenth from the people--that is, their brothers--even though their brothers are descended from Abraham.
  1. This man, however, did not trace his descent from Levi, yet he collected a tenth from Abraham and blessed him who had the promises.
  1. And without doubt the lesser person is blessed by the greater.
  1. In the one case, the tenth is collected by men who die; but in the other case, by him who is declared to be living.
  1. One might even say that Levi, who collects the tenth, paid the tenth through Abraham,
  1. because when Melchizedek met Abraham, Levi was still in the body of his ancestor.
  1. If perfection could have been attained through the Levitical priesthood (for on the basis of it the law was given to the people), why was there still need for another priest to come--one in the order of Melchizedek, not in the order of Aaron?
  1. For when there is a change of the priesthood, there must also be a change of the law.
  1. He of whom these things are said belonged to a different tribe, and no one from that tribe has ever served at the altar.
  1. For it is clear that our Lord descended from Judah, and in regard to that tribe Moses said nothing about priests.
  1. And what we have said is even more clear if another priest like Melchizedek appears,
  1. one who has become a priest not on the basis of a regulation as to his ancestry but on the basis of the power of an indestructible life.
  1. For it is declared: "You are a priest forever, in the order of Melchizedek." [1]
  1. The former regulation is set aside because it was weak and useless
  1. (for the law made nothing perfect), and a better hope is introduced, by which we draw near to God.
  1. And it was not without an oath! Others became priests without any oath,
  1. but he became a priest with an oath when God said to him: "The Lord has sworn and will not change his mind: `You are a priest forever.'" [2]
  1. Because of this oath, Jesus has become the guarantee of a better covenant.
  1. Now there have been many of those priests, since death prevented them from continuing in office;
  1. but because Jesus lives forever, he has a permanent priesthood.
  1. Therefore he is able to save completely [3] those who come to God through him, because he always lives to intercede for them.
  1. Such a high priest meets our need--one who is holy, blameless, pure, set apart from sinners, exalted above the heavens.
  1. Unlike the other high priests, he does not need to offer sacrifices day after day, first for his own sins, and then for the sins of the people. He sacrificed for their sins once for all when he offered himself.
  1. For the law appoints as high priests men who are weak; but the oath, which came after the law, appointed the Son, who has been made perfect forever.

Hebrews 9

  1. Now the first covenant had regulations for worship and also an earthly sanctuary.
  1. A tabernacle was set up. In its first room were the lampstand, the table and the consecrated bread; this was called the Holy Place.
  1. Behind the second curtain was a room called the Most Holy Place,
  1. which had the golden altar of incense and the gold-covered ark of the covenant. This ark contained the gold jar of manna, Aaron's staff that had budded, and the stone tablets of the covenant.
  1. Above the ark were the cherubim of the Glory, overshadowing the atonement cover. [1] But we cannot discuss these things in detail now.
  1. When everything had been arranged like this, the priests entered regularly into the outer room to carry on their ministry.
  1. But only the high priest entered the inner room, and that only once a year, and never without blood, which he offered for himself and for the sins the people had committed in ignorance.
  1. The Holy Spirit was showing by this that the way into the Most Holy Place had not yet been disclosed as long as the first tabernacle was still standing.
  1. This is an illustration for the present time, indicating that the gifts and sacrifices being offered were not able to clear the conscience of the worshiper.
  1. They are only a matter of food and drink and various ceremonial washings--external regulations applying until the time of the new order.
  1. When Christ came as high priest of the good things that are already here, [2] he went through the greater and more perfect tabernacle that is not man-made, that is to say, not a part of this creation.
  1. He did not enter by means of the blood of goats and calves; but he entered the Most Holy Place once for all by his own blood, having obtained eternal redemption.
  1. The blood of goats and bulls and the ashes of a heifer sprinkled on those who are ceremonially unclean sanctify them so that they are outwardly clean.
  1. How much more, then, will the blood of Christ, who through the eternal Spirit offered himself unblemished to God, cleanse our consciences from acts that lead to death, [3] so that we may serve the living God!
  1. For this reason Christ is the mediator of a new covenant, that those who are called may receive the promised eternal inheritance--now that he has died as a ransom to set them free from the sins committed under the first covenant.
  1. In the case of a will, [4] it is necessary to prove the death of the one who made it,
  1. because a will is in force only when somebody has died; it never takes effect while the one who made it is living.
  1. This is why even the first covenant was not put into effect without blood.
  1. When Moses had proclaimed every commandment of the law to all the people, he took the blood of calves, together with water, scarlet wool and branches of hyssop, and sprinkled the scroll and all the people.
  1. He said, "This is the blood of the covenant, which God has commanded you to keep." [5]
  1. In the same way, he sprinkled with the blood both the tabernacle and everything used in its ceremonies.
  1. In fact, the law requires that nearly everything be cleansed with blood, and without the shedding of blood there is no forgiveness.
  1. It was necessary, then, for the copies of the heavenly things to be purified with these sacrifices, but the heavenly things themselves with better sacrifices than these.
  1. For Christ did not enter a man-made sanctuary that was only a copy of the true one; he entered heaven itself, now to appear for us in God's presence.
  1. Nor did he enter heaven to offer himself again and again, the way the high priest enters the Most Holy Place every year with blood that is not his own.
  1. Then Christ would have had to suffer many times since the creation of the world. But now he has appeared once for all at the end of the ages to do away with sin by the sacrifice of himself.
  1. Just as man is destined to die once, and after that to face judgment,
  1. so Christ was sacrificed once to take away the sins of many people; and he will appear a second time, not to bear sin, but to bring salvation to those who are waiting for him.
